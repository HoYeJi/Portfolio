Mr. & Ms. Mauban High School

Mr. & Ms. Mauban High School is a highly anticipated and prestigious pageant held annually in the town of Mauban, a key highlight of the Maubanog Festival in July. This event not only showcases the beauty of the students but also celebrates their intelligence, creativity, leadership, and their advocacies. It is a vibrant and colorful competition that brings together the young minds of the community, allowing them to demonstrate their skills and personalities in a supportive and spirited environment. The pageant holds deep significance for both the students and the entire town, as it fosters a sense of pride, unity, and camaraderie.

Participating in this event is considered a tremendous honor, with students vying for the title while also gaining confidence, personal growth, and a platform to showcase their multifaceted abilities. The occasion is not just about beauty; it is about celebrating well-rounded individuals who excel in various fields, from academics to sports and the arts. The pageant has become a symbol of the community's values, highlighting the importance of holistic development and leadership.

The Maubanog Festival, taking place in July, adds a festive atmosphere to the pageant, as it coincides with other cultural activities, performances, and celebrations throughout the town. Families, friends, and townsfolk come together during this time to support the participants, making the event an unforgettable experience for everyone involved. It is an essential part of the town's heritage and an annual tradition that brings pride to the people of Mauban.

KALINGA: Kapit-Bisig sa Layuning Itaguyod ang Nutrisyon at Edukasyon ng Henerasyon Alpha

KALINGA is a community service project aimed at improving nutrition and promoting education for children and youth in Barangay 2, Plaza, Batangas City. Combining feeding programs, educational activities, and interactive games, it addresses food insecurity, fosters learning, and encourages sustainable practices.

Key Activities

Feeding Program: Nutritious meals for children.
Educational Sessions: Reading, health talks, and sustainability workshops.
Interactive Games: Team-building and cognitive skill activities.
Take-Home Resources: Seeds and learning materials to extend benefits.

Implementation

Dates: February 22 and April 12, 2025.
Participants: Children, families, local leaders, and volunteers.
Organizers: Batangas State University - Alangilan Campus students (CWTS - AL15).

Goals

Improve youth nutrition and health awareness.
Strengthen community bonds and teamwork.
Promote long-term healthy practices.

Expected Impact

Improved nutrition and health awareness among participants.
Strengthened community ties through collaboration and shared purpose.
Enhanced social, cognitive, and physical well-being of the youth.

My SDG Focus: Zero Hunger

SDG 2: Zero Hunger is a global initiative aimed at eradicating hunger, achieving food security, improving nutrition, and promoting sustainable agriculture by 2030. This goal strives to ensure that all people, especially the most vulnerable, have access to enough nutritious food year-round, while fostering the responsible management of our planet's natural resources.

The focus of SDG 2 includes ending malnutrition in all its forms, from undernutrition to obesity, through better access to healthy food. It aims to improve agricultural productivity and incomes, particularly for small-scale farmers, and advocates for sustainable farming practices that protect the environment and maintain biodiversity. By addressing key issues such as poverty, inequality, and climate change, SDG 2 seeks to create a world where no one goes to bed hungry and everyone has the opportunity to thrive.

Through initiatives such as reducing food waste, supporting local food systems, and promoting the development of resilient food infrastructures, SDG 2 brings together governments, organizations, and communities to work toward a sustainable future. Together, we can build a more equitable and food-secure world for all.
